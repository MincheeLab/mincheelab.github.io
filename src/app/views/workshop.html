What happened?

We were 15 "makers" from very different fields (electronics, developer, designer, video, game design...), all really motivated to create a new commuity in Macau!
We talked a lot, everybody get to introduce him/herself and we talked more ;-). We believe it is a really good start and we are eager to start the first workshop next month. This will be the real start on working/learning all together and hopefully we will start to push our imagination and creativity further to get some projects that could bring an exhibition by end of the year.

Interesting questions came up during the discussion, here are a few of them:

How do we become member of the lab?
Good question! We are still working on the association status as well as membership regulations/benefits and we hope they will be completed by next workshop and we can present them to all. Of course as usual, "rules are made to be changed"....
Can we meet more often if we are working on projects?
There is no top-to-bottom work in the community, therefore people working on a project can organize their own timing if they wish. However, we will try to organize a coffee time every month outside the workshop time to allow us to meet and talk about...whatever!.
Is there any rules for business companies to work with Minchee Lab on projects?
Again good question! Usually fab labs allow companies to use their resources to work on prototypes and most of the time a specific membership is required from them to allow them to support Minchee Lab running cost. Again this is something we hope we can present a first framework to be discussed soon.
I most probably forgot some stuff...you'd better come next workshop to experience the real thing!

"Make Your Own 3D Printer"

  • Sat November 22rd and Sun 23rd, 11am to 6pm; Optional: 23-24 Nov. 7pm to 10:30pm
  • Macau Science Centre [map | website]
  • maximum 15 seats
  • 5,500MOP (including the 3D Printer to take home)
  • English and Chinese.

Description

This workshop is very special since it will be much longer than normal...but super exciting! It is a collaboration between Minchee Lab and "This Is My City" Festival organized every year by +853 Assocation.

Everybody have heard about 3D Printers nowadays...but did you actually see one? use one? own one? build your own?
Here is your chance!

The 4 days workshop offers the participants the opportunity to build their own 3D printer through the guidance of Minchee Lab members. It will be a collaborative work to understand how a 3D printer works and how to use it at home. It will cover the assembling of all parts, the hardware and software configuration as well as the printing process and some basic of 3D modeling.
Participants will be encouraged to experiment and express their creativity in order to modify or create new objects. The possibilities are endless so join us for a unique experience!

Conference

Nov. 26th @ the Macau Science Center, Convention Center, Room 3-4
Open technologies such as open source software, electronics as well as digital fabrication through computer-aided manufacturing machines have, in the last 5 years, increase the potential of creativity for everyone. It is not anymore reserved to experts nor to well-funded companies to create what they always dreamed about. Through the democratization of technologies and collaborative places a new movement is born around the world: the maker movement.

This conference will look at this new transformative movement around the world with several key social aspects:

  • Academic and Education - what are the new research areas? What implication in the academic environment. How these new technologies and collaborative approaches can transform some of our educational programs?
  • Arts and Design - What are the new possibles for artists and designers?
  • Business - new opportunities, new processes, faster prototyping are some of the elements that could transform a lot of businesses, especially in the creative industries area.

Showcase

Nov. 27th @ the Macau Design Center

The 3D printers assembled during “Make Your Own 3D Printer” workshop will be showcased as part of an audiovisual performance, featuring video mapping and real-time electroacoustic music.

The audience will have the opportunity to see the devices in action while listening to an experimental composition produced live with sound samples captured from their mechanical elements.
